01 / The Brief

The Math

What it actually takes to come off 15 lb in 8 weeks

W-C-R-W-C-W-R. Estimated burn is roughly 360 kcal/day. Hitting your goal needs a deficit of about 938 kcal/day — aim to eat near 1,596 kcal/day, with most of that gap coming from food, not the workouts below.

This pace sits above the usual 1–2 lb/week guideline — workable, but prioritize recovery.

Keep protein around 153 g/day, sleep 7–9 hours, and check in with a doctor before you start.

08 / Before You Start

Notes

Weekly totals and the things worth remembering

~560kcal / week · strength
~470kcal / week · cardio
~1,030kcal / week · total burn

That's a real contribution, but a fraction of the ~6,500+ kcal/week deficit the 1.9 lb/week pace calls for. The rest comes from how you eat — a moderate, consistent reduction, not an extreme one.

  • If a weight breaks your form before the set is done, drop down — no shame in it as a beginner.
  • Stop and rest on sharp pain. Normal muscle fatigue is fine; pain isn't.
  • Hydrate, and aim for 7–9 hours of sleep — both matter for fat loss and recovery.
Not medical advice — consult a physician
