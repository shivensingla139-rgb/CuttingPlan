01 / The Brief

The Math

What it actually takes to come off 15 lb in 8 weeks

Estimated maintenance is roughly 2,534 kcal/day. Hitting your goal needs a deficit of about 938 kcal/day — aim to eat near 1,596 kcal/day, with most of that gap coming from food, not the workouts below.

This pace sits above the usual 1–2 lb/week guideline — workable, but prioritize recovery.

Keep protein around 153 g/day, sleep 7–9 hours, and check in with a doctor before you start.

Rules — every strength day

  • Rest 30–40 sec between sets — keep moving, don’t sit down between exercises.
  • Weight progression each set: 10 lb → 12 lb → 14 lb (unless otherwise noted).
  • Total strength session time: ~35–45 minutes.
  • Calorie burn per strength session: ~300–450 kcal depending on rest time and intensity.

06 / Wednesday · Sunday

Rest

Full recovery — no structured exercise

Rest days are not filler — they’re when your muscles repair and your body actually changes. The strength work only sticks if you give it time to.

  • Light stretching or a slow leisure walk is fine — nothing structured or intense.
  • Prioritise 7–9 hours of sleep on rest nights; this is when most recovery hormones peak.
  • Stay hydrated and hit your protein target even on rest days — muscle synthesis doesn’t pause.
Full rest — earn tomorrow

07 / Before You Start

Notes

Weekly totals and the things worth remembering

~1,050kcal / week · strength (3×, incl. leg day)
~600kcal / week · cardio (2×)
~1,650kcal / week · total burn

That’s a real contribution, but the bulk of your deficit still has to come from diet — a moderate, consistent reduction, not an extreme one. Strength days alone can hit 300–450 kcal; cardio days add another 270–400 each.

  • If a weight breaks your form before the set is done, drop down — no shame in it.
  • On lateral raises: keep them strict. No swinging, no momentum — these are easy to cheat and easy to hurt.
  • Stop and rest on sharp pain. Normal muscle fatigue is fine; pain isn’t.
  • Hydrate, and aim for 7–9 hours of sleep — both matter for fat loss and recovery.
Not medical advice — consult a physician
